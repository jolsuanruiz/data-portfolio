Sales Analytics Dashboard

An interactive dashboard for exploring sales performance over time.

Project Overview

In this project I created a dynamic dashboard that visualizes historical sales data for a retail company. The goal was to help stakeholders quickly identify trends, compare performance across regions and drill down into specific products or time periods.

  • Purpose: To provide decision makers with an easy‑to‑use tool for exploring sales metrics.
  • Objective: Build interactive charts and KPIs summarizing sales volume, revenue and growth.
  • Tools: Python, Pandas, SQL, Tableau
  • Duration: 3 weeks

Approach

The project followed a standard analytical workflow: data extraction, cleaning, analysis and visualization. I wrote SQL queries to extract raw transaction data from a database, then used Pandas to clean and aggregate the data into a tidy format. After exploring the dataset, I identified key metrics and designed a dashboard layout. The final dashboard was built in Tableau and allowed users to filter by time period, region and product.

Results & Insights

The dashboard revealed several noteworthy findings:

  • Seasonality plays a major role, with strong peaks during Q4.
  • The central region consistently outperformed others in revenue.
  • A handful of products accounted for most of the revenue, suggesting a Pareto distribution.

Reflection

Creating this dashboard reinforced the importance of clear communication and interactivity. While the underlying analyses were straightforward, designing a user‑friendly interface required iterative feedback from stakeholders. Next steps would include adding forecast capabilities and more granular product views.
